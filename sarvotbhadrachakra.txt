# How to create a Sarvatobhadra Chakra (SBC)

**What it contains (classical version):** a 9×9 grid (81 cells) that carries the **28 nakshatras (incl. Abhijit)** on the outer ring, plus inner rings for **Sanskrit letters (name syllable), 12 signs, and the 7 weekdays**. SBC is then used with **vedha** rules (forward / opposite / backward) to judge transits against your marked points. ([Wikipedia][1])

## Step-by-step construction

1. **Draw the grid**

* Make a perfect **9×9** square grid (10 lines each way). Think of it as **4 concentric “rings”**:
  outer ring (32 cells) → two letter rings → sign ring → central 3×3 for weekdays/planets. ([Saptarishis Astrology][2])

2. **Reserve the four corners (outer ring) for vowels**

* The **four outer corners** are **vowel (swara) cells**, not nakshatras. The remaining **28 edge cells** carry the **28 nakshatras**. ([Wikipedia][1])

3. **Place the 28 nakshatras around the outer ring (clockwise)**

* Use one standard orientation **consistently**. Classical instruction says: **start at the auspicious NE edge (Ishāna kona) and count clockwise** (some traditions start at NW; either is okay if you keep it consistent). **Abhijit is placed between Uttarāṣāḍhā and Śravaṇa.** ([Wikipedia][1])
* A commonly used **clockwise sequence** (starting at the **NE edge** after the corner) is:
  **Kṛttikā → Rohiṇī → Mṛgaśīrṣa → Ārdrā → Punarvasu → Puṣya → Āśleṣā → Maghā → Pūrvaphalgunī → Uttaraphalgunī → Hastā → Citrā → Svātī → Viśākhā → Anurādhā → Jyeṣṭhā → Mūlā → Pūrvāṣāḍhā → Uttarāṣāḍhā → Abhijit → Śravaṇa → Dhaniṣṭhā → Śatabhiṣā → Pūrvabhādrapadā → Uttarabhādrapadā → Revatī → Aśvinī → Bharanī → (back to Kṛttikā).**
  (If your school/book uses the NW-start template, keep that template—but **never mix orientations**.) ([Wikipedia][1])

4. **Fill the two inner “letter” rings (akṣara)**

* The **two rings just inside the nakshatras** hold the **Sanskrit alphabet** (vowels + consonants). These are used for **name-syllable (nāma-akṣara) vedha**—you’ll mark the first syllable/letter of the native’s name here. Exact letter layout varies by lineage; the key is that **all 50 letters are accommodated across those two rings.** ([Wikipedia][1])

5. **Place the 12 rāśis (signs)**

* The **next inner ring** carries the **12 signs** in order (one per cell, continuing clockwise). You’ll use these for **rāśi-vedha** checks. ([Wikipedia][1])

6. **Fill the central 3×3 (weekdays/planets)**

* Put **Śani (Saturn) in the exact center**. Around it, follow the classical SBC weekday layout: **Jupiter left, Sun/Mars right, Venus top, Moon & Mercury bottom** (these central 7 also correspond to **vāra** cells). ([saravali.github.io][3])

> At this point your static SBC “template” is ready for any native or event.

# How to plot for a given person (using their Moon’s nakshatra)

You’ll mark **five things** on the ready SBC: **Janma nakshatra**, the **Tara-counted sensitive nakshatras**, **name akṣara**, (optionally) **Lagna sign**, and **birth weekday**.

1. **Mark Janma (birth) nakshatra**

* Find the native’s **Moon’s nakshatra** at birth (your panchāṅga/astro-software).
* On the SBC outer ring, **shade/mark that exact nakshatra cell**—this is **Janma**.

2. **Mark the key Tara points (by counting through the 28-star belt)**

* From Janma, **count clockwise** through the **full 28-nakshatra cycle (including Abhijit)** and mark:

  * **Karma** = **10th** from Janma
  * **Saṅghātika** = **16th**
  * **Samudāyika** = **18th**
  * **Ādhāna** = **21st**
  * **Vināśa** = **23rd**
    (These are the **sensitive targets**: if a transit hits/vedhas these, expect notable results.) ([Wikipedia][1])

3. **Mark the name-letter (Akṣara)**

* Take the **first syllable/letter** of the person’s **usual name** (the one they’re commonly addressed by).
* Mark that **letter’s cell** in the **letter rings**—this enables **akṣara-vedha** readings. ([Wikipedia][1])

4. **Optionally mark Lagna & weekday**

* In the **sign ring**, mark the person’s **Lagna sign**.
* In the **central 3×3**, mark the **weekday** of birth (vāra cell). These enhance rāśi/vāra checks. ([saravali.github.io][3])

# Using it with transits (the quick operating rules)

1. **Where to look:** On any date/time, note each **transiting planet’s nakshatra** (Moon’s nakshatra changes daily; others move slower). When a transit lands on a nakshatra that makes a **vedha** to your **Janma/Karma/…** marked stars, you’ll read results.

2. **Vedha directions (how a star “aspects” in SBC):**

* **Opposite vedha:** straight across (vertical/horizontal).
* **Forward vedha:** the **diagonal ahead** (clockwise) to the next border star.
* **Backward vedha:** the **diagonal behind** (counter-clockwise).
  (Think of **opposite = stationary**, **forward = direct motion**, **backward = retrograde** logic.) ([saravali.github.io][4])

3. **What gets affected (by which ring) when that Janma star is hit:**

* **Akṣara-vedha (name-letter):** loss/setbacks;
* **Swara-vedha (vowel corner):** illness;
* **Tithi/Weekday-vedha:** fear/anxiety;
* **Rāśi-vedha:** strong opposition/obstacle;
* **Multi-vedha (many rings hit at once):** progressively severe (four = life-threat per classics). ([Wikipedia][1])

4. **Planet causing the vedha matters:**

* **Sun:** grief; **Mars:** loss of wealth; **Saturn:** pain/ailments; **Rāhu/Ketu:** obstructions;
* **Moon:** mixed happenings; **Venus:** fear from foes; **Mercury:** sharpens intellect; **Jupiter:** gains/relief. ([Wikipedia][1])

## Minimal checklist you can follow each time

1. Build the 9×9; corners = vowels; outer ring = 28 nakshatras (with Abhijit), **clockwise**. ([Wikipedia][1])
2. Inside: two **letter** rings → **12 signs** ring → **weekday/planet** 3×3 (Saturn center). ([saravali.github.io][3])
3. For a person: mark **Janma star**, **10/16/18/21/23** from it, **name-letter**, (opt.) **Lagna** & **weekday**. ([Wikipedia][1])
4. For any day: check if transits make **forward / opposite / backward vedha** to your marks; **read ring-type + planet-type** for effects. ([saravali.github.io][4])

### Notes & variations you’ll see in books/software

* Some authors show slightly different **letter layouts**; what matters is that **all 50 akṣaras** are present for name-vedha. ([Wikipedia][1])
